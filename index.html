Trigonometry Questions Required for Physics

🔹 Easy

1. A right triangle has one angle of 30°. If the hypotenuse is 10 cm, find the side opposite the 30° angle.

Hint: Use sin 30° = 1/2.

Answer: 10 × 1/2 = 5 cm

2. What is the sine of 90°?

Hint: It's the maximum value of sine.

Answer: 1

3. If cos θ = 0.6, find sin θ (θ in the first quadrant).

Hint: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Answer: sin²θ = 0.64 → sin θ = 0.8

4. Verify the identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Hint: Use unit circle or Pythagoras in right triangle.

Answer: Always true for any angle.

🔹 Moderate

5. A block is on an inclined plane at 37°. Find gravitational force component down the slope (mass = 5 kg).

Hint: F = mg sin θ

Answer: ≈ 29.49 N

6. A projectile is launched at 25 m/s at 60°. Find horizontal and vertical components.

Hint: Use vx = v cos θ, vy = v sin θ

Answer: vx = 12.5 m/s, vy ≈ 21.65 m/s

7. A pendulum makes a 10° angle with vertical, length = 2 m. Find horizontal displacement.

Hint: x = L sin θ

Answer: ≈ 0.347 m

8. A force of 50 N acts at 45°. Find the vertical component.

Hint: Fy = F sin θ

Answer: ≈ 35.36 N

🔹 Challenging

9. Wave: y(x, t) = 0.05 sin(4πx - 200πt). Where is displacement always zero?

Hint: sin = 0 when argument is nπ

Answer: x = n/2, n ∈ ℤ

10. SHM: x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ). When is velocity zero?

Hint: v(t) = -Aω sin(ωt + φ)

Answer: t = (nπ - φ)/ω

11. Show radial acceleration a = v²/r

Hint: Use velocity vector change direction in circular motion

Answer: a = v²/r from limit of Δv/Δt

12. Two waves: y₁ = A sin(kx - ωt), y₂ = A sin(kx - ωt + φ). Find resultant amplitude.

Hint: Use identity: sin α + sin β = 2 sin((α+β)/2) cos((α−β)/2)

Answer: Amplitude = 2A cos(φ/2)
